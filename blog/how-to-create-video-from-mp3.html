How to create a video from an MP3 file

YouTube requires video files — you can't upload an MP3 directly. If you want to share a music track, podcast episode, or audio recording on YouTube, Instagram, or TikTok, you need to wrap it in a video container. Here's how to do it for free.

Why YouTube doesn't accept MP3

YouTube is a video platform and only accepts video file formats (MP4, MOV, AVI, etc.). To upload audio-only content — a song, a podcast episode, a meditation, a spoken word recording — you need to wrap the audio in an MP4 container, even if there's no visual content.

The simplest approach: a static image (a photo, album cover, waveform graphic, or black screen) combined with your audio track, packaged as an MP4 video.

Tips for YouTube uploads

For a better YouTube video, consider pairing your audio with a relevant image or thumbnail. While TurboConvert creates a minimal MP4 container, tools like CapCut or DaVinci Resolve (both free) let you add an image or animated background before export.
