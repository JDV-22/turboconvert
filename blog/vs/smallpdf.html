TurboConvert vs Smallpdf

Smallpdf is one of the most well-known PDF tools online — but its free tier is heavily restricted: 2 tasks per day, file uploads to their servers, and a paywall for anything more. TurboConvert offers a genuinely unlimited free alternative. Here's the full comparison.

The 2-tasks-per-day problem

Smallpdf's free tier allows just 2 conversions per day. If you need to compress a PDF, then convert another, you've hit your limit before 9am. To continue, you must either wait 24 hours or pay for a subscription starting at around €9/month.

⏱️ Real scenario: you need to compress 5 scanned PDFs for a job application. With Smallpdf free, you can do 2 today and must wait until tomorrow for the rest. With TurboConvert, you compress all 5 in one sitting — no limit, no account, no waiting.

TurboConvert has no task counter, no cooldown timer, and no paywall. Use every tool as many times as you need.

What TurboConvert does better

  • Truly unlimited for free. No daily caps, no task counters, no waiting. Use it 50 times a day if you need to.

Where Smallpdf still has an edge

  • PDF editing and annotation. Smallpdf offers in-browser PDF editing, commenting, and signature tools that TurboConvert doesn't have yet.
  • Larger file support. Smallpdf handles files larger than 100 MB on paid plans.
  • Enterprise features. For teams needing SSO, API access, or admin controls, Smallpdf's business plan is more mature.
