How to convert JPG to PNG for free

Most of the time, JPG is the better format — smaller files, universal compatibility. But sometimes you need PNG: for transparent backgrounds, for editing without quality loss, or for graphics that include text and sharp edges. Here's when and how to convert JPG to PNG for free.

When to convert JPG to PNG

  • You need a transparent background. JPG doesn't support transparency. If you need to remove a background from a photo, you must save it as PNG.
  • You're editing the image multiple times. Re-saving a JPG introduces more compression artifacts each time. PNG is lossless — save it 100 times with no quality degradation.
  • The image contains text or logos. JPG compression blurs sharp edges. PNG keeps them crisp.
  • You need the exact original pixels. For medical imaging, document scanning, or any use where pixel accuracy matters, PNG is the right format.

Will the PNG be larger than the JPG?

Yes — PNG files are typically 2–5× larger than equivalent JPG files for photographs. This is expected: PNG uses lossless compression (no data is discarded), while JPG uses lossy compression. For graphic elements, icons, and text-heavy images, the difference is smaller.
