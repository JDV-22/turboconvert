How to convert Word to PDF for free — no Word required

You need to convert a Word document to PDF but don't have Microsoft Word installed — or you're on a device that doesn't run it. This guide shows you how to do it for free, in your browser, on any device.

Why convert Word to PDF?

PDF is the universal document format — it looks identical on every device, operating system, and screen size. Word documents can render differently depending on the version of Word, the fonts installed, and the operating system. Converting to PDF guarantees your layout, fonts, and formatting are preserved exactly.

  • Job applications: CVs and cover letters should always be sent as PDF.
  • Contracts and forms: PDF prevents accidental editing.
  • Presentations: PDFs open instantly without needing PowerPoint or Keynote.
  • Email attachments: PDFs are universally readable and typically smaller than DOCX files.

How to convert Word to PDF without Word

What about formatting?

TurboConvert preserves most formatting elements including headings, paragraphs, tables, and font styles. Complex layouts with custom fonts or advanced Word features may have minor differences, as TurboConvert uses LibreOffice WebAssembly rather than Microsoft's rendering engine.

For documents with critical formatting (contracts, branded reports), check the result and compare with the original. For standard documents like CVs, letters, and reports, the output is typically identical.
