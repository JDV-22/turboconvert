How to convert WebP to JPG for free

WebP is Google's modern image format — efficient for websites but poorly supported in some image editors and older software. If you've downloaded a WebP image and can't open it, converting to JPG takes seconds. Here's how.

What is WebP and why can't some apps open it?

WebP was developed by Google in 2010 as a replacement for JPG and PNG on the web. It offers about 25–34% smaller file sizes than JPG at equivalent quality, which is why many websites now serve images in WebP format.

However, WebP support in desktop applications hasn't kept pace with browser support. While all major browsers display WebP natively, many image editors (including older versions of Photoshop, Paint, and GIMP) don't open WebP files without plugins.

Does converting WebP to JPG lose quality?

Converting from WebP to JPG introduces a small quality reduction because JPG uses different compression than WebP. At standard quality settings (80–90%), the difference is nearly invisible in photographs. For images where you need to preserve every detail, stay in WebP if your software supports it, or use PNG as a lossless alternative.
